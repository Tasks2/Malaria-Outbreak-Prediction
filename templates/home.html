Malaria Prediction System

Harnessing Weather Data to Predict and Prevent Malaria Outbreaks in Malindi

What is Malaria?

Malaria is a disease caused by a parasite transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes. By using data like temperature, humidity, and rainfall, we can predict potential outbreaks.

Why Malaria is a Major Concern in Malindi?

Malindi, a coastal town in Kilifi County, experiences high malaria prevalence due to its warm and humid climate, which creates a perfect breeding ground for mosquitoes. Factors such as heavy seasonal rainfall, stagnant water bodies, and increasing temperatures contribute to frequent outbreaks, putting thousands of residents at risk.

How Our System Helps

Our Machine Learning-Driven Malaria Prediction System utilizes weather data—including temperature, humidity, and rainfall—to predict potential malaria outbreaks in Malindi. By analyzing these environmental factors, we provide early warnings and help local health authorities and residents take preventive measures before an outbreak occurs.

Key Features of the Malaria Prediction System

  • Real-time Data Analysis – Uses historical and current weather patterns for accurate predictions.
  • Localized Insights for Malindi – Specifically designed to track malaria risks in Malindi and surrounding regions.
  • Early Warning System – Alerts health officials and residents to take action before outbreaks spread.
  • Actionable Recommendations – Provides guidance on preventive measures, including mosquito control and healthcare strategies.
